Lions, emblems of the East African country, are in sharp decline. Only 2,000 remain, compared with 20,000 fifty years ago. This is due to the fact that populations are killing them to defend their crops and livestock. To reduce vengeance by poisoning, as well as poaching and other assaults on wildlife, we are testing a new approach, based on the involvement of local communities. Local people are hired as guards, but they can also act as mediators in the event of conflict, or as environmental defenders. Numerous pacts have been signed with the Masai to protect local wildlife. Numerous aids are made available to them to contain the growing flow of cattle every year. By 2021, the number of lions in Kenya had risen slightly to 2,500. This figure is constantly rising, thanks to the efforts of the entire Maasai community.

What can we do to protect lions ?

  • We can learn to the population to respect them ;
  • We can too purchase poachers ;
  • We can also define some national parks reserved to them ;
  • We can make rules to avoid mass tourism ;
